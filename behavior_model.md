# Aflac Behavior Model — Distilled from 10 Years of Guidance vs. Actuals

**Analysis as of: 2026-05-14**
**Evidence window:** 2015 – Q1 2026
**Sources scored:** 22 documents — FAB books 2015-2024, 10-Ks 2020-2025, annual reports 2015-2019, earnings transcripts Q1 2024 – Q1 2026
**Sample:** 589 forward-looking statements extracted; 183 of those have clean numeric guidance scored against actual outcomes

> **Important currency notes:**
> - The hit/beat/shortfall figures below cover full-year actuals through 2025. 2026 is in-progress — only Q1 2026 results are known as of this analysis. Statements about "2026 tracking" reflect a single quarter, not a verified full-year outcome.
> - The BEAT/SHORTFALL pattern is calibrated to **numeric guidance**. Qualitative claims like "we feel good about persistency" are present in the master ledger but cannot be scored against numeric actuals — apply judgment, not the percentage pattern, when interpreting qualitative statements.
> - Pre-LDTI (2015-2022) and post-LDTI (2023+) numbers are not always directly comparable. Where the pattern below cites BEAT/SHORTFALL counts, both eras are pooled because the *behavior* (sandbagging on margins, missing on growth) is consistent across both — but specific numeric values should not be transposed across the LDTI break without checking.

Use this as context when interpreting Aflac management commentary. When the user asks "what does it mean when Max says X?" or "is this guidance conservative?" — the patterns below are the answer.

## Headline pattern

**Aflac BEATS its own numeric guidance about 3.5x as often as it SHORTFALLS.** Across 183 scorable statements: 59% BEAT, 14% IN_RANGE, 17% SHORTFALL, 10% MIXED. This is structural conservatism, not lucky variance. When you read an Aflac guidance range, the prior is that actuals will land at the favorable end or beyond.

## Pattern catalog

### 1. Capital ratio targets are floors, not forecasts
- SMR Japan target ">500%": BEAT 13/14 times.
- RBC US target ">400%": BEAT 11/12 times.
- Capital deployment (sub. dividends + buybacks): BEAT 25/26 times.
- **Interpretation:** When management cites a capital ratio target, read it as the minimum they'll defend, not the expected outcome. Actual capital is typically 100-200% above the stated floor.

### 2. Margin guidance is sandbagged
- Pretax margin (Japan + US combined): 14/15 BEAT, zero SHORTFALL.
- Benefit ratios (Japan + US): 19/42 BEAT (came in *lower* / better), 7/42 SHORTFALL.
- Expense ratio Japan: 6/15 BEAT, 1/15 SHORTFALL.
- **Interpretation:** Margin/ratio ranges typically include cushion. Expect actual to land at the favorable end of the band.

### 3. Growth and ROE guidance is where misses live
- Adjusted ROE: 0/6 BEAT, 6/6 SHORTFALL. Aflac has *never* hit a stated ROE aspiration in the dataset.
- Sales growth: 7 BEAT vs. 8 SHORTFALL. Coin flip with downside skew.
- Third sector / cancer sales growth: 0/4 BEAT, 2/4 SHORTFALL.
- **Interpretation:** Multi-year growth aspirations are the most optimistic part of Aflac's guidance. The longer the horizon, the larger the gap. Discount aspirational ROE/growth targets accordingly.

### 4. US expense ratio is the recurring sore spot
- US expense ratio: 2/16 BEAT, 5/16 SHORTFALL — the only major metric where SHORTFALLS outnumber BEATS.
- Driver: sustained platform/digital investment 2020-2025 and growth-business scaling costs.
- **Interpretation:** When Aflac guides US expense ratio, the bias is toward the upper end of the range. "We continue to scale new business lines" is the recurring tell.

### 5. Register language is calibrated and meaningful
- Baseline adjectives: "solid" and "strong." Used dozens of times per call across decades.
- "Excellent" is rare. When it appears, the underlying metric is typically already running well ahead of plan.
- **Interpretation:** Verbal escalations are confirmation of an already-locked-in beat, not a forward signal. A drop from "strong" to no adjective is more meaningful than the appearance of "excellent."

### 6. Post-LDTI guidance is meaningfully tighter
- Pre-LDTI FAB books (2015-2022): wide ranges, frequent multi-year aspirations, more BEAT outcomes.
- Post-LDTI (FAB 2024 + 2024-2026 transcripts): narrower ranges, more IN_RANGE outcomes, shorter horizons.
- The 2024 FAB pivoted from the long-running 2025 5-year glide path to a 2025-2027 three-year window.
- **Interpretation:** Recent guidance is less likely to sandbag. Expect tighter beat margins going forward; "BEAT" outcomes in 2024+ are smaller in magnitude than 2015-2022 vintages.

### 7. Capital return commitments are the most reliable signal in the dataset
- 43 consecutive years of dividend increases through 2026.
- Dividend growth guidance: hit/beat 4/4.
- Capital deployment totals: 25/26 BEAT.
- **Interpretation:** When Dan Amos commits to capital return, treat it as near-certainty. This carries more predictive weight than any growth claim.

### 8. Japan guidance is closer to a forecast; US guidance is closer to a stretch goal
- Aflac Japan: 51% BEAT, 21% IN_RANGE, 15% SHORTFALL.
- Aflac US: 54% BEAT, 13% IN_RANGE, 21% SHORTFALL.
- Japan's mature persistency and stable benefit ratio make beats easier to predict.
- US is in active investment mode; SHORTFALLS concentrated in expense ratio + growth metrics.
- **Interpretation:** Apply more skepticism to US guidance, especially on expense ratio and growth.

### 9. When Aflac quietly drops a target, it usually means they won't hit it
- FAB 2020/2021/2022 all repeated the same 2025 targets: US $1.8B new sales, US persistency 79-80%, Japan ¥80B sales.
- FAB 2024 did not reaffirm any of them — and pivoted to a different 2025-2027 frame.
- The "Operating EPS growth 2-7% ex-yen" target prominent in FAB 2015 disappeared after FAB 2017 mid.
- **Interpretation:** Track what's missing between FAB books, not just what's added. A quietly retired multi-year target is almost always a leading indicator of a miss.

### 10. Internal reinsurance is the rare explicit self-graded "win"
- FAB 2022 goal: "lift adjusted ROE by 100-200 bps over time" through internal reinsurance.
- FAB 2024: "We have already achieved the 100 to 200 basis points run-rate ROE enhancement."
- **Interpretation:** This rare instance of explicit self-grading suggests internal reinsurance is a safe execution play, not a stretch ambition. Treat related forward statements with confidence.

## How to use this in answers

When a user asks about a recent earnings statement:
1. Identify which pattern bucket the statement falls into (capital, margin, growth, expense, etc.).
2. State the management quote / guidance plainly.
3. Add the relevant pattern context. Example: "Max guided benefit ratio of 60-63% for Japan in 2026. Historically Aflac's benefit ratio guidance comes in at the favorable end of the range — 19 of 42 prior guidance instances BEAT, only 7 SHORTFALL — so absent a specific reason to doubt, expect actual to come in toward the low end."
4. Flag if a metric is in a pattern bucket where Aflac systematically misses (US expense ratio, ROE aspirations, multi-year growth).

Don't claim a specific number unless it's in the transcripts. The patterns are interpretive context, not a substitute for the underlying quotes.
